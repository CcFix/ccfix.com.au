Laptops · Battery

Laptop battery,
replaced properly.

When a laptop starts needing the charger far more often than it used to, a fresh battery is often the simplest way to bring it back to life.

A tired battery is one of the most common reasons a perfectly good laptop starts to feel like more trouble than it's worth. The good news is that, in most cases, a quality replacement battery solves the problem cleanly — without needing to replace the whole machine.

Signs a replacement is worth considering

  • A full charge lasting a fraction of what it used to
  • Sudden shutdowns, often well above 0% remaining
  • The base of the laptop running noticeably warm
  • A case that's started to lift, bulge, or sit unevenly — a sign of swelling

How we approach it

We check the battery's actual condition first — not just the symptoms — explain plainly what we find, and fit a quality replacement matched to your laptop's exact model where that's the sensible move. We finish by confirming charging behaviour and battery reporting are both back to normal.

On swollen batteries

A swollen laptop battery is worth bringing in sooner rather than later — we handle these carefully and safely, every time.
