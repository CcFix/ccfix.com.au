Camera playing up?
Let's take a proper look.

Blurry shots, a black screen in the camera app, or a lens that's taken a knock — camera trouble is more common than people think, and usually well within fixing.

Your phone's camera does a lot of quiet work — video calls, quick snaps, scanning documents, the lot. So when it starts misbehaving, it tends to get noticed fast. The good news is that most camera issues come down to one of a handful of causes, and most of them are repairable without replacing the whole phone.

What we typically see

  • Photos that come out blurry, hazy or oddly tinted
  • The camera app showing a black screen or an error message
  • Focus that hunts back and forth and never settles
  • A cracked lens letting light leak into the shot

How we approach it

We check whether the trouble sits with the lens, the camera module itself, the small ribbon cable that connects it, or simply a setting that's gone astray — because there's no point replacing a part that was never the problem. Once we know what's actually wrong, we carry out the repair carefully, then run the camera through its paces — stills, video, focus and flash — before handing it back.

A word on parts

We fit quality replacement components suited to your exact model, and we'll always tell you plainly what's gone in and why. No guesswork, no mystery parts.
